OVERVIEW

Just as the School Admission counsellors want the admission management system to be user-friendly, efficient, and effective along with wanting to be able to easily access, update, and track student information we want them to experience the same flexibility and control when it comes to fulfilling their target conversions. The Newton Admission Module provides an overall streamlined and efficient admissions process that helps schools to attract and retain more students, as well as improve the overall counsellor experience.

This is my story of designing the “Newton Admissions” - base module of Newton Classroom, an ERP that empowers administrators to experience hassle-free Admission Processes.

BACKGROUND

What is Newton?

Just as the School Admission counsellors want the admission management system to be user-friendly, efficient, and effective along with wanting to be able to easily access, update, and track student information we want them to experience the same flexibility and control when it comes to fulfilling their target conversions. The Newton Admission Module provides an overall streamlined and efficient admissions process that helps schools to attract and retain more students, as well as improve the overall counsellor experience.

My Role

As UX/UI Designer , I worked with multiple cross-functional teams that included 2 product managers, 3 Front-end developers, 4 backend developers, and Customer Excellence Representative, to redesign and deliver the module. The designs were monitored and timely reviewed by design Lead Vaibhav Vardhan.
Responsibilities during the process include contextual research, product documentation, interaction design, visual design, and handoff document. The final deliverables include multiple rounds of product discussion, development walk through, and design iterations.

Project Summary

This project took place between April 2022 and August 2022. The 5-month deadline was crucial to follow since academic admissions start in the month of October and our launch date was finalised to 15th Oct 2022.
The application was earlier designed in 2021 however it was not shipped due to functional errors occurrence. Hence the key goal of this update are to find faults in the current flow and work on improving them.

Why we decided to launch the admission module first

Admission management is a critical feature for any school and also in a school ERP application because it plays a key role in the enrolment process. It helps manage the flow of applicants, organize and track important student information, and automate many of the administrative tasks associated with admissions.
Overall, an admission management module can help counsellors to manage and organize the admissions process more effectively and efficiently, while also providing insights and data to improve the process and make better decisions.

CHALLENGE

Objective:

Before we start, let's recollect the key factor behind putting Newton on the market and the problem we tried to solve.

In our Traditional school management system the biggest problem that a school face are the prevalence of paper-based systems. Almost all out there do a lot of paperwork and it goes without saying that they are highly burdened by the same. In such a situation it is natural that we would find it really difficult to maintain the data and records in proper order.

This is where Newton comes as the solution. We built extraordinarily efficient applications to help schools maintain systematic processes and get organized management solutions.

However, despite all digital work administrators still approach manual or on-paper methods for various internal processes. This leads to various problems namely- Unorganized admission communications & parent interactions Delayed timeline due to the level of dependencies Unorganized progress tracking

Which results in the Loss of valuable customers, affecting the Counselor’s Performance too

So here we will be discussing about How we can solve the above problems by going through the approach we followed and the exercises we did in order to understand the user’s pain points and validate design decisions.

Increase accountability of Admission Counsellors

Since pandemic, there has been a rapid change in the school ecosystem with the consumption of digital management where both parties expect and are more inclined towards the efficient medium for high system status visibility.
This inherently means that we are not redoing the entire experience from scratch. Instead, we are improving core experiences and UI wherever necessary such that development can be done in 8-10 weeks and pushed to production
Our challenge was to evolve with the ecosystem and solve the ground-level users’ needs, which include improving efficiencies and accountability of Admission Counsellors at school and optimising the entire Admission process. Also keeping the flow non-intimidating for non-tech savvy Admission counsellors.
The design aesthetic needed to be updated to fit Newton's new style guide.

User Goals

Primarily we should help the schools in creating a better first impression with parents. This would mean that parents should experience a seamless process during Admissions with high system status visibility and increased branding content..

Business Goals

Ensuring all designs are to be delivered in a timely manner to minimize boost costs.

Iterative High Fidelity Wireframe

After the brainstorming session with the design lead it's time to get the wireframe done. We run through iterative wire-framing by creating multiple versions of screens to be redesigned to finalize the layout and incorporate new features. The intent of this approach was to create a User-Centered Design that would address the needs and goals of all counsellors.

1. Dashboard Experience

The initial task was to develop a Statistical Dashboard that would provide an overview of key performance indicators related to conversion. The design was simple, but the tricky part was figuring out which content to present.. The dashboard was revamped by reorganising how information was presented, incorporating a quick task glance feature with additional details.
Considerations taken into account when redesigning the dashboard included.

  • We updated the user interface by changing the colours to create a more modern look and more effectively convey the value of Newton, resulting in improved customer satisfaction.
  • Made key performance indicators more easily visible to users.
  • Graphical statistics have been included to evaluate the admission procedure.
  • The widgets are designed to be multi-functional and provide metrics for all possible scenarios.

2. All Student Landing Page

The next redesign focused on the landing screens which serve as a central location for all candidate information.
The redesign of the candidate detail experience was carried out after careful consideration, which included:

  • Navigation was added to enable easy switching between different stages of the admission process, which helps to reduce the time needed to access student information at each stage.
  • Visual differences were introduced in all landing screens to enable easy identification of the screen type.
  • By using the Progressive Disclosure property on the table, data is distributed in a way that makes it easy for counsellors to analyse the information they need.

3. Candidate Profile Page

The candidate profile view allows counsellors to monitor and keep records of all relevant information related to the admission process. While redesigning this screen, emphasis was placed on ensuring Visibility Property, as counsellors needed to be able to take quick action.
The information is presented in four cards, each of which displays important data related to the candidate, including:

  • Candidate Details - For each candidate, a specific Identifier is provided which gives information and attributes related to the admission process. Additional profile-related information can be accessed by utilising the call to action provided.
  • Admission Process - Given the layout changes, I suggested implementing an efficient method for users to navigate through the different stages of the admission process. Since the information density is low for all stages, it is feasible to consider adding UI elements to enhance the click experience.
  • Followups - The follow-up card feature was included based on user requirements. It is essential for the follow-ups to be visible on the candidate's profile as it helps to address delays or issues that might occur.
  • Notes - Given the significance of monitoring all forms of communication throughout the admission process, we have incorporated a notes widget on the profile screen, enabling counsellors to easily take notes during conversations with parents.

4. Global Appointments Page

This area shows all appointments set for the day for every AC and stage, which aids in more effective planning for that day, particularly for interactions with the Principal.
It is important for counsellors to keep track of appointments separately from other activities. Therefore, the new proposed layout separates the appointments into two sections: active and closed appointments.

5. Introducing Followups

Follow-ups are important in the school admission management process for several reasons. First, it allows the school staff to keep track of the status of a candidate's application and ensure that all necessary steps have been completed. Additionally, it serves as a way to communicate with the candidate and their family, which can be critical for ensuring that the process goes smoothly. Following up also allows the school staff to identify and address any issues that may arise, such as missing documents or incomplete forms. Finally, it serves as a way to ensure that the school is meeting its enrolment goals and that the candidate is aware of the next steps in the process.

This section displays all follow-up plans scheduled for the day for each AC, which helps them efficiently call back parents and update the status on Newton.

6. Other Experiences

Profile Details

This view provides a timeline of all events, including all minor details such as the date of enquiry, the application date, the school visit date and more.

Candidate Communications

The administrator can access a list of all historical messages (both SMS and Email) sent to the candidate, which can be viewed through the candidate's profile.

Activity Log

To enable counsellors to track all actions and events that occur within the application or system during the admission process, a dedicated space needs to be kept for this purpose.

Reject/Droff-off Candidate

The administrator can change the candidate's status to Rejected, Drop-off, or return it to Active.

PRELIMINARY USER TESTING

When the design is partly implemented , I conducted few user testing to verify that the design successfully communicates the user requirements and experience, and also identifies some usability issues that could be improved in the future.

Major Finding

The modern use of color of the application is well-liked by all participants, regardless of their gender, who find it "well-organized," and "aesthetically pleasing.”

  1. Users are requesting to see the type of inquiry (web/walk-in) as it would assist the marketing team in planning their campaigns.
  2. Despite the filters being in place, most applicants find it challenging to access information about candidates who have been successfully admitted.
  3. Some areas needed UI changes to reduce user confusion and minimize the number of clicks required.

THE IMPACT

Although the service is still in its early stages, it has exceeded our expectations. Since its launch, it has become a model for other schools and has attracted 3 more schools to adopt Newton Classroom based on the prototype we used for demonstration. Despite the rapid growth, we focused on enhancing key metrics such as engagement, retention, adoption, and acquisition. Post-launch release notes were implemented to enhance the user experience.
